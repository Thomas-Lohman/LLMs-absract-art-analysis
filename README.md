# LLMs for Abstract Art Analysis

This project explores how large language models (LLMs) and vision-language models (VLMs) interpret and describe abstract art. In domains like art analysis, where meaning is inherently subjective, the central challenge is aligning model-generated reasoning with human interpretation and emotional understanding. This work is in the area of studying model alignment in subjective domains, and is used to evaluate in-context learning capabilities to determine whether fine-tuning may be necessary in future research.

The project combines data collection, multi-model generation, and embedding-based evaluation to analyze how closely AI-generated descriptions align with human perspectives on abstract visual content.

---

## Project Overview

Abstract art presents a uniquely difficult task for AI systems due to its lack of explicit structure and objective meaning. This project investigates:

- How different models interpret abstract visual content  
- The effectiveness of zero-shot and few-shot prompting strategies  
- The degree to which model outputs align with human-written descriptions  

---

## Key Ideas

- In-context learning as a baseline for subjective reasoning tasks  
- Cross-model comparison of LLMs and VLMs  
- Embedding-based similarity for evaluating alignment with human interpretations  
- Motivation for future fine-tuning or alignment-focused approaches  

---

## Notes

- This project is notebook-based and designed for exploratory research  
- Some models may require keys or local access to reproduce results

---

## Takeaway

This work demonstrates that while modern LLMs and VLMs can generate plausible interpretations of abstract art, their ability to consistently align with human reasoning is limited. The results motivate further research into fine-tuning and alignment techniques for subjective, non-deterministic domains.
